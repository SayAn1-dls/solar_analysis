# Load Shifting And Demand Response

Flexible industrial loads, water pumping, HVAC pre-cooling, and EV charging can be scheduled to align with forecasted solar availability. This reduces reliance on grid imports and improves renewable utilization.

Best practices:
- Move discretionary loads toward hours with predicted solar surplus.
- Delay non-critical consumption during expected deficit windows.
- Separate critical demand from flexible demand so optimization does not endanger essential operations.
- Use daytime surplus periods for pre-heating, pre-cooling, or charging tasks that can be performed early.

Operational note:
Demand response should be transparent to operators and include fallback logic when live conditions differ from the forecast.
