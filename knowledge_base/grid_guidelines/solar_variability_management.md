# Solar Variability Management

Solar generation is highly sensitive to cloud cover, panel temperature, and morning-evening ramp transitions. Operators should watch for steep downward ramps and preserve reserve capacity before the ramp begins rather than after the drop is visible in telemetry.

Best practices:
- Identify hours with rapid forecast decline and pre-position battery discharge or fast-start support.
- Treat sunrise and sunset periods as transition zones with higher uncertainty than midday peaks.
- Use short forecasting horizons for dispatch decisions and combine them with operator review.
- Keep a conservative reserve margin when the forecast indicates instability or sharp irradiance change.

Operational note:
When forecast variability is high, flexible loads should be shifted away from risk windows so critical demand is protected first.
