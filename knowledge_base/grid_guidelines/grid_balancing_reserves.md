# Grid Balancing Reserves

Renewable-heavy systems need reserve planning because generation can change faster than demand. Operators should maintain a reserve margin sized for forecast uncertainty and the speed of possible output drops.

Best practices:
- Maintain operating reserves above the forecasted demand floor when solar uncertainty is elevated.
- Schedule grid import or backup generation ahead of predicted deficit windows instead of reacting late.
- Review the largest forecast shortfall and prepare balancing resources for that interval.
- Increase caution when several risk factors overlap, such as high variability and rapid ramp-down.

Operational note:
Reserve recommendations are advisory inputs and should be checked against grid codes, contractual limits, and equipment health.
